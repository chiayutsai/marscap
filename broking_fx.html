Margin Requirement

    When setting up a new position

  • FX:Necessary Margin = Open Price × Contract Size × 1%
  • Commodity CFD: Necessary Margin = Open Price × Contract Size × 2%
  • Securities CFD: Necessary Margin = Open Price × Point Value × 2%

    When holding a position at the time of rollover

  • FX:Projected Margin =Close Price× Contract Size × 1%
  • Commodity CFD: Projected Margin = Close Price × Contract Size × 2%
  • Securities CFD: Projected Margin = Close Price × Point Value × 2%
  • During rollover, projected Margin will be the necessary margin for the outstanding position in the next trading day.
  • If it’s a long position, close price should use ask price.
  • Necessary Margin of Hedge Position： If the long position and the short position of the same product are held at the same time, necessary margin will be calculated by the higher open price between the long and the short position.
  • Please Refer to the contract size and point value in Product Outlines and Trading Hours

Margin Calculation

  • Current Margin Percentage = Effective Margin/ Necessary Margin
  • Projected Margin Percentage = Effective Margin / Projected Margin
  • Effective Margin= Balance±Floating P/L
  • Variation Margin= Effective Margin- Necessary Margin

Auto Settle

    Rules of Auto Settle of Sport Products:

    Spot Products involves currency pairs of FX trading, spot gold and spot silver of Commodity CFD.

  • Between the trading hours from Monday to Friday, Auto settle starts if current margin percentage drops below 25%.
  • At the moment of market close on Friday or a certain trading day before a holiday, auto settle also starts if the projected margin percentage drops below 100%.

    Rules of Auto Settle of Futures Products:

    Future Products involves crude oil. copper, soybean, wheat, corn and all the products of Securities Futures CFD.

  • Between the trading hours from Monday to Friday, Auto settle starts if current margin percentage drops below 25%.
  • Between the market close and the next trading day’s market open from Monday to Thursday, auto settle starts if the projected margin percentage drops below 50%.
  • At the moment of market close on Friday or on a certain trading day before a holiday, auto settle also starts if the projected margin percentage drops below 100%.

    Rules of Auto Settle if the outstanding positionsinclude both spot and futures products：

  • If the outstanding positions include both spot and futures products, the auto settle rule should refer to both of the above simultaneously
  • Auto settle starts automatically at the real-time price until the effective margin meets the requirement.
  • For two or more outstanding positions, auto settle adheres to the principle of last in, first out.
  • Auto settle provides no guarantee that the client’s loss can be exactly controlled at a price equivalent to the set amount of auto settle. It’s likely that the execute price is more unfavourable for the client than the price of auto settle in theory if the market price fluctuates violently and against the client, which may cause the deficit in the book account and require margin call.

What is Rollover

Rollover is when the delivery day is extended to the next trading day in the settlement.
For FX trade in the interbank market, delivery day is t+2
For FX margin trading, the actual delivery can be infinitely extended through rollover, which can be generated with no commission charge. The FX margin trading involves spot gold CFD and spot silver CFD.

What is Swap Interest

Swap interest is the interest accrued from interest rate difference occurred when two currencies traded during the rollover.
If one buys a currency with a higher interest rate and sell a currency with a lower interest rate and holds the position to the next trading day, the trader can collect interest, and vice versa.

  • Swap interest accrues from FX outstanding positions held at rollover, and will be debited or credited in the account balance;
  • Swap interest also accrues on holidays.
  • Since the interest rate depends on a country’s economic conditions, it will be adjusted at any time.

What is Interest Days

The interest day is determined based on the difference of delivery date between buying and selling instead of the difference of transaction date.
With the t+2 rules, if the outstanding positions at a rollover from Wednesday to Thursday, the actual delivery rate is respectively on Friday and next Monday, which is the reason that rollover on Wednesday needs to pay interest of 3 days.

  • the rules of the interest day in a week maybe vary with the conditions.

Adjustment on Expiration Date

If one holds futures CFD positions, when the old month contract expires, positions will be transfer to the new month contract automatically based on the closing price of the new and old contracts. The adjustment will be reflected in the clients trading account on the expiration date.

    Adjustment Calculations

  • When Holding Long Position
    Adjustment= [–(Ask Price of New Contract –Ask Price of Old Contract) ×Tick Value] × Lot(s)
  • When Holding Short Position
    Adjustment= [+ (Bid Price of New Contract –Bid Price of Old Contract) ×Tick Value] × Lot(s)

Limit Order and Stop-Loss Order of the Futures CFD

The price change will be larger when the old contract of futures CFD is transferred to the new one. The set price of limit order or the Stop-Loss Order may be traded immediately, which possibly different from the price specified by the clients.
